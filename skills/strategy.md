# /strategy

Strategic analysis for any decision. Structures options, evaluates tradeoffs, and produces a clear recommendation.

## Trigger
When the user says: "strategy for [X]", "strategic analysis", "help me decide", "what's the play here?", "options for [decision]", "how should I approach [X]"

## Workflow

### 1. Understand the decision
Ask (if not already clear):
- What's the decision or situation?
- What constraints exist? (time, budget, people, politics)
- Who are the stakeholders and what do they care about?
- What happens if you do nothing?

### 2. Map the options
Identify 3-4 realistic options (always including "do nothing" or "status quo").

### 3. Evaluate tradeoffs
For each option, assess:
- Upside potential
- Downside risk
- Effort/cost
- Time to impact
- Stakeholder alignment

### 4. Produce the analysis

```
## Strategic Analysis: [Decision]

### Situation
[2-3 sentences on context and why this decision matters now]

### Options

| Option | Upside | Downside | Effort | Timeline |
|--------|--------|----------|--------|----------|
| A: [name] | [benefit] | [risk] | [H/M/L] | [timeframe] |
| B: [name] | [benefit] | [risk] | [H/M/L] | [timeframe] |
| C: [name] | [benefit] | [risk] | [H/M/L] | [timeframe] |
| D: Status quo | [benefit] | [risk] | None | N/A |

### Recommendation: [Option X]

**Why:** [2-3 sentences — the core argument]

**Key risk:** [the biggest thing that could go wrong]

**Mitigation:** [how to reduce that risk]

### Next Steps
1. [Immediate action with owner]
2. [Second action with timeline]
3. [Decision checkpoint / review date]
```

## Rules
- Always include a "do nothing" option — sometimes that's the right answer
- Never present more than 4 options (decision paralysis)
- Take a stance — this is a recommendation, not a neutral summary
- Be honest about uncertainty and what you don't know
- If the decision is urgent, lead with the recommendation and support after
- Keep total output under 500 words
