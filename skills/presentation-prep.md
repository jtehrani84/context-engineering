# /presentation-prep

Prep for any talk, demo, or presentation. Produces audience analysis, flow structure, talking points, and delivery coaching.

## Trigger
When the user says: "presentation prep", "prep for my talk", "demo prep", "I'm presenting [topic]", "help me prep for [event]"

## Workflow

### 1. Understand the context
Ask (if not clear):
- What are you presenting? (topic, product, idea)
- Who's the audience? (role, expertise level, what they care about)
- How much time do you have?
- What's the one thing you want them to remember?
- Is this persuasive (convince them) or informational (teach them)?

### 2. Structure the presentation

```
## Presentation Prep: [Topic]
**Audience:** [who]
**Duration:** [time]
**Goal:** [one sentence — what should they DO after this]

### Audience Analysis
- They care about: [their priorities]
- They're skeptical of: [likely objections]
- They already know: [don't waste time on this]
- They need to hear: [the gap you're filling]

### Flow (with time allocation)
1. **Hook** (10%) — [specific opening that grabs attention]
2. **Problem** (20%) — [frame the challenge in THEIR terms]
3. **Solution** (40%) — [your content, structured for impact]
4. **Proof** (20%) — [evidence, demo, or example]
5. **Close** (10%) — [specific call to action]

### Key Talking Points
- Point 1: [headline] — [supporting detail]
- Point 2: [headline] — [supporting detail]
- Point 3: [headline] — [supporting detail]

### Transitions
- Hook -> Problem: "[specific transition phrase]"
- Problem -> Solution: "[specific transition phrase]"
- Solution -> Proof: "[specific transition phrase]"
- Proof -> Close: "[specific transition phrase]"

### Objection Handling
- If they say "[objection]": respond with "[pivot]"
- If they say "[objection]": respond with "[pivot]"

### Delivery Notes
- Open with: [specific first 2 sentences — memorize these]
- Energy peak: [where to increase intensity]
- Pause moments: [where silence creates impact]
- Close with: [final words — memorize these]
```

## Rules
- The audience's priorities come first, always
- One core message. If you can't state it in one sentence, it's too complex.
- Talking points use their language, not yours
- Include specific transition phrases (not just "then move to...")
- Delivery notes are coaching, not content narration
  - BAD: "This section presents the challenge..."
  - GOOD: "PAUSE after the number. Let them react. The goal is tension."
- Keep total prep under 500 words
