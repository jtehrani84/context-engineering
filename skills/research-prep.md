# /research-prep

Pre-meeting or pre-work intelligence gathering. Run before any meeting, deep work session, or engagement to have structured context.

## Trigger
When the user says: "research prep", "prep for meeting", "prep for [topic/company/person]", "meeting with [name]", "what do I need to know about [X]"

## Workflow

1. **Identify the subject** — ask if not provided (person, company, topic, or project)
2. **Gather intelligence:**
   - Subject overview (what they do, their context, recent developments)
   - Your relationship history (check memory, wiki, past interactions)
   - Relevant background (competitive landscape, industry trends)
   - Open questions or gaps in your knowledge
3. **Structure the output:**

### Output Format

```
## [Subject] — Research Prep
**Date:** [today]
**Context:** [meeting/project/engagement]

### Snapshot
- What: [brief description]
- Recent: [1-2 relevant headlines or developments]
- Size/scope: [if applicable]

### Your History
- Last interaction: [date + what happened]
- Key contacts: [names + roles if available]
- Open threads: [anything unresolved]

### Their World (What They Care About)
- [Top priority #1]
- [Top priority #2]
- [Relevant challenge or initiative]

### Your Angle
- [What you bring that maps to their priorities]
- [Relevant proof point or reference]
- [Potential area to explore]

### Meeting Agenda Suggestion
1. [Opening question — about THEM, not you]
2. [Discovery area]
3. [Value point to demonstrate]
4. [Next step to propose]
```

## Rules
- 70% their world, 30% yours
- Lead with THEIR context, not your capabilities
- Include a specific opening question that proves you did homework
- If data is unavailable, say so — never fabricate
- Keep total output under 400 words
- Check wiki/people/ and memory for existing context first
